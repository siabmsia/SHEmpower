Abortion

- An overview of the history of abortion -

Abortion is a medical procedure commonly used to deliberately terminate a pregnancy, such that it doesn't result in the birth of a child. Abortion is usually done in two different ways: medication abortion where the patient takes medicine to end a pregnancy (which is often known as “abortion with pills”) or in-clinic abortion where the patient undergoes surgical procedures (which is known as “surgical abortion”). Abortion is a very safe medical procedure. According to Planned Parenthood[1], about 1 out of 4 women have an abortion procedure by the time they are 45 years old. ABORTION:A HISTORY

Induced abortion procedures have been around for a long time (We refer to these procedures as induced abortion due to the fact that naturally occurring events that lead to the termination of a pregnancy is known as a miscarriage, although the singular term “abortion” is commonly used to ). There has been a history of many different abortion techniques with the intention of terminating the pregnancy, such as the administration of abortifacient [2] herbs, the implementation of sharpened objects, the application of abdominal pressure, and so on. The enforcement of abortion laws has fluctuated over various time periods. Abortion has been practiced since ancient times, yet the legality and availability is questioned and threatened by many anti-abortion groups, who libel fundamental women's rights. While a lot of abortions rights movements led to the abolishment of many abortion bans in the 20th century and while abortion does remain legal in many Western countries, many people, who deem themselves “pro-life” question the legality of abortion rights.
